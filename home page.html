Global Warming in US:

Over the past 24 years, global warming has led to significant climate changes in the U.S., including rising temperatures, more frequent and intense heatwaves, wildfires, and extreme weather events like hurricanes and floods. Average temperatures have risen by around 1.8°F (1°C) since the late 20th century. The western U.S. has experienced prolonged droughts, while sea levels have risen along the coasts, increasing flood risks. The changing climate has also disrupted ecosystems, agriculture, and water supplies, affecting daily life. These trends have accelerated in recent years, driven by increased greenhouse gas emissions from human activities.
