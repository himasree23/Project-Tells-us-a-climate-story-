US Data from 2016-2020

This is the data for the years 2016 to 2020.

Average Temperature Increase: The U.S. experienced an average temperature rise of about 1.8°F (1.0°C) from 1901 to 2020, with significant increases noted in the 2015-2020 period. 016 was the warmest year on record until surpassed by 2019. In 2015, U.S. CO2 emissions from fossil fuels were approximately 5,300 million metric tons By 2019, this number had decreased to about 5,100 million metric tons, indicating a decline of roughly 3.8% over that period. Atmospheric CO2 Levels: Atmospheric CO2 concentrations reached about 414 parts per million (ppm) in 2020, up from about 400 ppm in 2015, highlighting the ongoing increase in greenhouse gases.
